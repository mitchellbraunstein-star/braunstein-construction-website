Our Mission

We help Toronto homeowners and small landlords add gentle density — legally, beautifully, and profitably.

Toronto's housing rules just changed. Under By-laws 847-2025 and 849-2025 and Ontario Regulation 462/24, garden suites, laneway suites, and multiplexes up to four units are now permitted as-of-right in most residential zones — and the City waives development charges on multiplexes up to six units. Braunstein Construction is a second-generation, family-owned builder that exists for exactly this moment: turning underused backyards and single-family lots into safe, beautiful, code-compliant homes for renters, family members, and future generations.

What We Build

Services built around Toronto's new zoning

Every project we take on adds housing supply where it's needed most — your own lot. From feasibility to occupancy, we handle it all under one roof.

Garden Suites

Detached backyard homes that don't need a laneway. As-of-right in most Toronto residential zones under By-law 847-2025. Typical size: 600–1,000 sq ft, fully self-contained.

Laneway Suites

Two-storey rental units on lots backing onto a public laneway. We handle site servicing, fire access, and the Committee of Adjustment when needed.

Multiplex Conversions

Convert a single-family home into a duplex, triplex, fourplex, or sixplex. Toronto waives development charges on multiplexes up to six units — direct savings of $50K–$200K+ per project.

Multi-Family New Builds

Ground-up purpose-built rental: townhomes, low-rise rental buildings, and infill multiplexes across Toronto and the GTA. We work with architects you choose or bring our own.

Whole-Home Renovation

Full gut renovations, second-storey additions, and major remodels — often combined with adding a basement apartment or garden suite to maximize the value of the property.

Common Questions

What Toronto owners ask us most

Straight answers about garden suites, multiplexes, and the rules that just changed.

A garden suite is a detached, self-contained residential unit built in the backyard of an existing home. Unlike a laneway suite, it does not need to face a public laneway — access can run from the street through the side yard or along the main house. Under Toronto By-law 847-2025, garden suites are now permitted as-of-right in most residential zones (R, RD, RS, RT, RM).
Most Toronto residential lots can now support a multiplex of up to 4 units as-of-right. In sixplex-permitted areas, you can build up to 6 units plus a garden suite — for a total of 7 dwellings on a single lot. The City of Toronto waives development charges entirely on multiplex projects up to 6 units.
No car parking is required for garden suites, laneway suites, or multiplex conversions in Toronto. You do need to provide 2 bicycle parking spaces.
As of 2026, expect $400–$550 per square foot all-in (design, permits, construction, and finishes) for a code-compliant garden suite in Toronto. A typical 700 sq ft, one-bedroom garden suite lands between $280,000 and $385,000. Multiplex conversions vary widely depending on the existing structure — we provide a fixed-price quote after the feasibility review.
From signed contract to occupancy, a typical Toronto garden suite takes 8–12 months: roughly 3–5 months for design and permits (faster now that suites are as-of-right) and 5–7 months for construction. Multiplex conversions take 9–14 months depending on scope.
We serve the City of Toronto — including Etobicoke, North York, Scarborough, East York, and York — and the Greater Toronto Area including Mississauga, Vaughan, Markham, and Richmond Hill.
